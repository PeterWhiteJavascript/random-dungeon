Project Summary
New Game

In the beginning of the game, the player has many options they can choose from to customize their start.

^
Storyline

The player finally reaches his 18th birthday, which means he's ready to go out into the world and work. His grandfather agrees to purchase a farm somewhere and the player gets to choose what type of farm it is. The player is given three options for world generation based on what activity they'd prefer. The activities are Mining, Farming, and Cooking. The player then travels to a bazaar type event with his grandfather where he can purchase certain things from all of the shops there. The player's grandfather gives him 30000 coins to purchase items. This starting scene is scripted and all of the available items are set. Once the player is ready to travel to the farm he bought, he just needs to talk to his grandfather who is waiting at the bazaar entrance.

Grandpa suggests to buy certain items based on the type of farm bought. He also pays the material cost for any Kitchen, Barn or Chicken Coop, or Crafting Room that is bought. The player doesn't have to take his suggestions.

Mining - Buy Hammer, Shovel, Wooden Sword, Leather Armour, and Blacksmith Crafting (Value 25500).

Farming - Buy Hoe, Watering Can, 18 Turnip Seeds (540), 24 Potato Seeds (960), Beginner Chicken, and 20 Pieces of Chicken Feed (500) OR Beginner Cow/Sheep + 20 Pieces of Animal Feed (1000) (Value 19500 OR 25000). Chicken Feed is worth 25, Animal Feed is worth 50, Turnip Seeds are worth 30, Potato Seeds are worth 40.

Cooking - Buy Beginner Baker, some easy recipes, and some ingredients for the recipes (Value 25000 + recipes + ingredients).

In the final map, the player reaches the bottom of the mine and meets the final boss. The boss says to compete with him in a cooking contest. Once the player defeats him in the cooking contest, the player has to fight the boss in combat. After that, the game is won.

World/Map Generation
Worlds (sometimes referred to as maps) in this game consists of randomly generated maps that have progression based on what is generated. For example: a map that has a dungeon on it may have some kind of story arc to do with the dungeon.
Chunks are pieces of the randomly generated map (world). They consist of tiles, objects, and interiors, as well as many other custom properties. There are many different types of chunks as well as two different ways of generating them.
The player can travel to different maps by buying a different farm. Each map has one farm on it (the one that you buy). When acquiring a farm, the player gets a "map" item that can be viewed from the menu that gives an overview of what has been explored in the world. Maps (the item) start out blank and are filled out as the player explores the world.
^
What is a Map?

A map is a scene where the player can walk around in. The main map, which is displayed when the player travels to a farm, contains information about the tiles and objects that are displayed, as well as other information about the world. Other maps are simply the interiors of structures. Maps consist of two tile layers that contain the terrain, and one object layer that contains all objects that have a physical location on the map.

Here are the properties that are kept in the world object:

Name Description
timesVisited The number of times the player has visited the world. This could be useful for putting more popular worlds at the top of the list or something.
level The level of the world. Many randomized things are based off this number. Higher level worlds tend to have more to do as well as better items.
music This is an object that contains information about what music to play and when. This information is based on the world's theme.
info Keeps track of certain information that has happened in the world. This info is displayed when talking to certain NPC's. For example, when the map is generated, the info property stores how many lakes there are, as well as if there is an island on any of there lakes, as well as if there are any dungeons on any of the islands. There is an NPC that talks about the lakes so that you don't actually have to explore them to know certain things about them.
levelData This stores information about the theme of the map as well as its width and height. The theme mainly has to do with the tile indexes of certain tile types (dirtTile = 43, etc..). It also contains the data for what objects should spawn along the road.
chunks Any chunks that spawn objects are tracked here so that they can be respawned when neccessary.
map Contains both tilelayer matrixes, the objects that need to be spawned, and an array that contains special ground such as dug ground and tilled soil.
playerSpawn The location where the player should spawn when entering the map.
name The name of the world. It is randomly generated based on the level of the world and its features.
festivals Keeps track of the day and type of all festivals that can occur in the world.
Premade and Random Chunks

Chunks can be generated by selecting from a list of pre-made chunks, or by creating the tiles from a set of instructions.

Premade chunks are usually smaller so that they blend in with the main map better. Exceptions to this are towns and farms. Players may end up seeing the same town generated in different maps, but the objects inside the town will likely be different (different shops, etc...).

Random chunks are what make each map really unique. They can be randomly generated lakes, rivers, dungeons, and more. Since the shape, objects that spawn, and even interiors are different in each map, the player should always have something new to explore.

There are a few different properties that can be set for most chunks.

Name Description
w The width of the chunk.
h The height of the chunk.
minDistFromEdge The minimum distance from the edge of the map to the closest side of the chunk.
minDistFromFarm The minimum distance from the farm.
maxDistFromFarm The maximum distance from the farm.
minDistFromTown The minimum distance from town.
maxDistFromTown The maximum distance from town.
pathThickness The radius of the road that connects to this chunk.
tiles An array of tile indexes that correspond to ground tile layer 1.
tiles2 An array of tile indexes that correspond to ground tile layer 2. Chunks that don't use this layer can omit it.
playerSpawn This is the coordinate where the player spawns after entering the map
spawnObject An array of objects that get spawned on this chunk every 7 days. The item data includes the tile index, a minimum - maximum for how many are spawned, a chance to spawn for each object that may be spawned, a location for if there is only one that needs to be spawned at a specific location, and the treasure grid number for chests. If there are multiple of the same loc set for spawnObjects, the first one that is spawned is kept. This allows for sometimes spawning rarer items on the same tile if an earlier spawnObject chance fails.
spawnCharacter An array of characters that should be spawned on this chunk. Keep in mind that interiors are chunks as well, so this property is mostly used for those. The properties that can be added are the sprite, location, direction facing, and dialogue type.
door The coordinates and interior numbers the doors on the chunk.
exit The coordinates for the chunk's exit which is attached to the road. If there is no exit set, a random location will be generated. If there are multiple exits, the closest one will be selected
interior The interior of a door that is on the chunk. Interiors are actually chunks as well, so they can have all of these same properties. Since they are not on the main map, they don't have any sort of relative placement and are placed at [0, 0]
Types of Chunks
Interior

Interiors are the insides of the doors that are placed on chunks. They can be houses, dungeons, shops, or the farm.

Farm

The farm is where the player spawns when traveling to a world. Depending on the type of world, the exterior could feature large plots of land, a cave, a garden, a pond for raising fish, weeds spawns, mushroom logs, a barn, etc.. The interior could feature a kitchen, different processing machines (milk -> butter, eggs -> mayonaise, etc...), a hot tub (for replenishing energy midday), etc...

If the farm comes with a barn, there can be different animals that can be bought for it in the town. Caring for animals is simplified in that they don't have any sort of affection level and the quality of produce is determined by the character's proficiency. To milk cows, you just need empty bottles, which can be found by digging, be bought in stores, or recycled from using an item stored in a bottle.

The farm chunk connects to most other chunks that are generated. It does not connect to the town as the town has some mutual connections with the farm. This means that instead of a straight path from the farm to the town, it will go farm -> forest <- town, and farm -> river <- town.

Town

In the town, the player can do many things such as do things in shops, talk to villagers which increases the town relationship (which leads to unlocks), find items, etc...

The town connects to the river, forest, and ocean, which means that all maps must have at least one of these.

All shops are random within each town. There must be one seed shop as well as a tool shop that sells at least Watering Can, Hoe, Axe, and Hammer. All other shops can be randomly selected from the list of possible shops for the world level.

Plot

An additional plot of soil that you can farm on.

House

Contains a house that can be added to randomly generated chunks such as forests and lakes. This house selects from a different list than what is found in town. These houses tend to be less important, but still pretty cool. Examples could be hiring certain characters, oracle that reveals certain information about the map, taste tester that will buy one cooked item off of you each day and give feedback based on how it tastes, etc...

Dungeon

Contains a randomized dungeon. The top and bottom floors are not random. The top floor contains a set of stairs going down into the random part, as well as a sign that displays some information about the dungeon such as deepest floor reached, treasure at bottom taken, rocks smashed, ore mined, tiles dug, items found in ground, total floors traveled, etc...

There are different depths and treasure grids based on the level of the dungeon, which is based on the level of the world.

Sometimes you can come across special floors within the dungeon that have a little scene play. Something like you encounter a witch that can cast a spell to send you down 5 floors, you find a group that wants to fight, you find a healing spring that heals your party a bit, etc...

The bottom floor always contains at least one treasure chest for the taking. Sometimes there is a boss at the bottom that you have to fight on higher level dungeons.

River

A randomized river that can be horizontal or vertical. It always has a bridge across it at least 3 tiles from the side. There are objects that can be spawned as well as a few custom properties that can be set.

Name Description
len The length of the river. The river ends up being a bit shorter than len since each iteration moves the river either forward or to the side.
riverDirection Horizontal or vertical.
riverThickness How wide the river is.
bank How wide the ground around the river is.

All Maps

There are three types of main maps: Mining, Farming, and Cooking; each with a different focus for making money. To go to another map, the player has to buy a farm on it from the farm shop.

Each map type has base chunks and has more added in higher level maps. Different worlds have different activities that should be focused on to maximize profits. For example, a mining world has little farmland, but many dungeons, so having a crafting room and exploring mines is better than trying to farm.

All worlds have some areas to collect forage items. If the player completely runs out of money and they lose their tools somehow, they should be able to make money from doing forage tasks or part-time jobs. The game shouldn't be able to be soft-locked by not having any money.

Mining

The mining world contains multiple dungeons that can be explored. Since monsters can be found in dungeons, it is necessary to have a blacksmith shop where the player can buy weapons and armour. The main sources of income are battling, selling ores, and selling crafted items.

Farming

The farming world contains extra farmland as well as more selection of seeds in the shop. The shop also sells fertilizer. The main source of income is selling produce in the farmer's market.

Cooking

The cooking world contains a much larger town than the other two. There is a food kiosk in town where people can register to have food ready by a certain time. The main sources of income are working part time at a restaurant, selling dishes at the food kiosk, and selling dishes at your own shop.

Each world has a goal that when reached gives the player a prize as well as unlocks the next level of world. Goals include winning a festival, taking the treasure at the bottom of a dungeon, clearing a certain number of enemies, defeating a boss enemy, selling a certain number of dishes in a restaurant, selling a certain number of crops at the farmer's market, catching a certain rare fish, etc...

Tools and Player Actions
The player can perform different actions depending on which tool is equipped. Tools must be registered to a hotkey in the menu to be used. The player can pick up certain items off the ground and any item that can be picked up can be put away in the bag or thrown. The player can also interact with some objects while not holding anything.
^
Tools

Tools are used to perform most actions in (farmgame). They are usually initialy bought in shops and upgraded at the blacksmith in exchange for money and ore. Tools do not break or wear out, but it is still necessary to have many of the same type of tool since helpers need them to perform their tasks.

Here is a list of all of the tools that can be acquired.

Name Value Quality Description
Hoe 2500 AOE, Range, Energy Cost The hoe is used to till soil to make it ready to plant seeds in.
Axe 2500 Damage, Energy Cost The axe is used to chop stumps which turns them into lumber. The lumber sells at a higher price than sticks and can be used for more crafting recipes. Lumber can also be used as a weak barricade in battle.
Hammer 2500 Damage, Energy Cost The hammer is used to process rocks into material stone. It can also be used to smash ore rocks in the dungeon.
Watering Can 2500 AOE, Range, Capacity, Energy Cost The watering can is used to water crops. It can be filled in any body of fresh water. There is usually a water filling station on the farm.
Fishing Rod 3500 Energy Cost, Damage, Speed The fishing rod is used to catch fish. It can be used when there is a fish animation on water. It only casts at 1 range, so the player must have a raft to get deeper fish. Damage and Speed affect the fishing minigame.
Raft 1000 Speed Rafts are used to cross fresh water. The player can pick up items and fish while on it. It must be crafted from lumber. Since it is a tool, it is carried around in the bag just like a regular item.
Shovel 2500 Energy The shovel is used to dig up certain ground tiles. There may be items in the ground. This is the easiest way to acquire coins.
Other Actions
Picking Items up off the Ground

You can pick up most 1x1 items from the ground. All items that can be picked up can go in the bag. It doesn't cost any energy to pick up items. When forage items are picked up for the first time, their quality is generated base on the character that picked it up.

Taking Items out of the Bag

Items can be removed from the bag in the menu by setting an item to a hotkey and then pressing the hotkey while out of the menu and not holding anything. If there is space in front of the player, a stack of items can be placed on the ground from the menu as well. This is the only way to place a stack of items as throwing an item only places one of the item.

Throwing Items

Any held item can be thrown as long as there is space in front of the character. Items cannot be thrown on tiles around the outside edge of the map, as if the map has space to go offscreen, that's where the doorway is. Any items that have been thrown stay there on any map that is saved until they are picked up again. This means that any items thrown on the ground in a random dungeon are lost if the player leaves the room.

Interacting with People and Objects

To interact with people and objects, press the action button (SPACE). This will either pick up an item, throw an item, or talk to an NPC or sign.

Items
There are many different types of items and ways to use them. Items refer to the objects that characters can pick up and put in their bag.
All items may be sold in some way as well. Depending on the item type, it may need to be sold in a restaurant, in the farmer's market, etc... The price that an item fetches is based on its value.
^
Item Types

There are 8 different item types. Each item can only have one type. Item storage is based on the item's type.

Equipment

Equipment can be obtained by purchasing from a blacksmith, crafting, or finding in treasure chests. Equipment increases stats, as well as provides different skills in battle. See equipment for more details.

Tool

Each tool allows the player to perform a different action. They can be obtained by buying in a shop or crafting. See tools for more details.

Ore

Ores are mined in dungeons, found in caves, or bought at a specialty shop. They can be used to craft equipment. Ore can also sell at a high price, though finding someone who will buy them is difficult. They can be traded for prizes at the dungeon shop. See mining for more information.

Material

Materials are obtained by processing rocks and logs in the overworld, or picking up pebbles and sticks. Materials are used to craft buildings and are required when purchasing a new farm. See crafting for more information.

Forage

Forage items are wild items that are generally found on the ground. These include weeds, flowers, and wild fruits. See foraging for more information.

Produce

Anything that is grown on soil, or produced by animals is considered produce. This includes crops, tree fruits, dairy, ingredients for cooking, etc... See farming for more information.

Seed

Any seed pouches are the seed type. These are mainly acquired by buying them in a shop or using the seed make to produce them. See seeds for more information.

Dish

All cooked items are of the dish type. All dishes can be eaten. There are many different "sets" of dishes that give more health and energy than the dishes individually. See cooking for more information.

Item Value and Quality

An item's quality affects its value. At maximum quality (10), a item is worth double its base value. The formula for calculating an item's value is: 

                                                    let value = Math.ceil(item.value + item.quality * item.value / 10);

Item quality is set when an item is acquired for the first time. Normal shops always sell the lowest quality items. See character stats for more details on quality and consistency. The formula for calculating an item's initial quality is: 

                                                    let range = Math.max(0, charQuality - charConsistency);
                                                    let bottom = Math.max(1, charQuality - range);
                                                    let quality = ~~(Math.random() * range) + bottom;

Tools quality affects their efficacy. Better quality tools tend to require less energy to use and can have longer range and greater area of effect.

Time and Weather
Weather affects crop growth and temperature can increase the character's energy use. Each season has different values for the random weather, which is what makes it rain more in the fall, as well as snow in the winter.
^
Time

Time passes at one minute per second. Time influences which shops are open, which type of fish are available to be fished, as well as festival starts and ends. The temperature changes throughout the day on the hour.

End of Day

The end of day happens when all party members run out of health, or the player decides to sleep in a bed. There are many things that happen at the end of day.

Weather

The weather is random each day and is generated a week in advance. It can be influenced by certain magic that some people posses later in the game. The player can know the next day's weather by tuning in to the radio. The weather affects the rate at which crops grow.

Weather Types

Hot Sun - Adds 2 sun to each crop.

Sun - Adds 1 sun to each crop.

Rain - Adds 1 rain to each crop.

Heavy Rain - Adds 2 rain to each crop.

Temperature

Each day, there is a temperature value (in Celsius) generated alongside the weather. On very cold and very hot days, the player's energy use can be increased. Each hour, the temperature may change.

Thresholds

(30C and above) - Use +1 energy on all actions

(15C - 29C) - Neutral

(5C - 14C) - Use -1 energy on all actions (Minimum 1)

(-9C - 4C) - Use +1 energy on all actions

(-10C and below) - Use +2 energy on all actions

Character Stats, Bag, and Storage
Characters have different stats which the game checks against to see how well a character performs a certain action. In addition to normal stats such as energy and health, there is also proficiency which contributes to attaining higher quality items. Each character has their own bag, so having more party members means you have more carrying capacity. You can have up to three party members at a time. Additional members can be on standby or tasked to a job as a helper.
^
Common Stats
Energy and Health

Energy is depleted when doing an action with a tool. Once the energy bar is completely empty, the character's health gets depleted at twice the rate for the same actions. Once the character runs out of health, they black out and wake up at the farm the next day with very little energy and health restored. Health is also what is depleted when an enemy attacks the character. If a character still has stamina when their heath bar reaches zero, they still black out.

Strength

The attack power of the character. Is used in the calculation for how much damage is done to rocks and logs for processing, as well as how much damage is done to enemies in battle.

Bulk

The defense of the character. Makes enemies do less damage in battle.

Speed

The run speed of the character as well as animation speed for tool use. Also contributes to the turn order in battle.

Increasing Stats

To increase stats, a character must level up from battle. To level up, they need to get a certain number of EXP. Points from winning battles. Stats can also increase from reaching specific tool proficiencies. For example, at level 3 Axe, the character gets +1 Strength.

Special Stats
Qualities

A character's quality stats are what is checked when determining the quality of an item that is about the be acquired. These stats are improved by increasing the proficiency of the corresponding tool or action.

The quality of an item is determined by the character's consistency stat as well, which improves with most actions taken. See item quality for more details.

This gives a number of at least 1 between the consistency and the quality. This means that the charQuality is the maximum quality that can be generated for the item. If the character's consistency is equal to or greater than the quality, the item will be generated with the maximum quality.

Name Description
Forage Quality Quality of foraged items. Generated when the item is picked up for the first time.
Cooked Quality The maximum quality of cooked dishes. Even if the player is using high quality ingredients, the final product will max out at this value.
Fish Quality The quality of fish. Generated when the fish is obtained from fishing.
Ore Quality The quality of ores that have been dug up in a dungeon. Generated when the ore is produced after smashing a rock.
Material Quality The quality of materials that are produced when processing an object.
Tool Quality The quality of tools when smelted in the forge.
Consistency The minimum number that all qualities are based on. This is overridden if the maximum quality is lower that this value.
Proficiency

Each character has their own proficiency with each tool. This value improves as they use the tool. Once a proficiency reaches a certain value, the character's special stat that corresponds to the proficiency goes up in level. Each level takes longer than the last to level up. The minimum level is 1.

The formula for getting the number of points needed to reach the next level is: 

                                                    let pointsNeeded = (base + (currentLevel * currentLevel - 1) * 0.75 * base);

Example (base = 100): 100, 325, 700, 1225, 1900, 2725, 3700, 4825, and 6100

Farming
Farming is a valuable source of raw materials for cooking, income, as well as health and energy restoration items. To grow crops, the player need access to a hoe, watering can, seeds, and available soil. To care for livestock, a barn or chicken coop, animals, and animal feed is required. Livestock can be butchered for meat, though meat can also be bought from a butcher.
^
Seeds
Obtaining Seeds

1. Purchase from a shop in town. Seeds are always low quality. This is the best way to obtain a lot of seeds to grow crops where the quality doesn't matter as much.

2. Given to the player for completing a request. Seeds vary in quality depending on the rank of the request. This is the only way to obtain certain seeds early on.

3. Produce by putting a crop in the seed maker. Seeds quality is based on the crop quality. The seed maker is not available until the player owns a pretty expensive farm.

4. Dig up from the ground using the shovel. Seeds quality is based on the character's shovel rank. The seeds that can be dug are the ones sold in the shop.

Planting Seeds

To plant seeds, the player must use them on tilled soil. Crops have a 25% chance of withering every day after they have not been watered for 3 days straight. This prevents players from continuing to increase a crop's quality with fertilizer while not advancing the crop.

Fertilizer

Crops can be fertilized once per day, which increases their quality by 0.25. Fertilizer can be crafted with weeds or can be bought in a shop.

Crops
Harvesting Crops

When a crop is done growing, the player can interact with it while not holding anything to pick it. For simplicity, all crops are harvested this way.

There is a small chance of increasing the crop's quality depending on the character that is harvesting it. This chance is increased depending on how high the character's special cropQuality stat is.

Uses for Crops

1. Some crops may be eaten to restore energy and health.

2. All crops can be used in recipes to create dishes.

3. Some NPC requests can require the player to give them a certain crop of a certain quality (or higher).

4. There are crop festivals where the player must bring their highest quality crop to compare with NPC's to see who has the best crop.

Fishing
Fishing is a great way to gain energy restoring items while away from the farm. Fish can be sold in a fish market, used as in dishes, or eaten raw. Fish are more active at different times of the day, and an animated sprite appears on a tile where a fish inhabits. See Fishing Derby for information about the fish festival.
^
How to Fish

To fish, the player needs a fishing rod. Better quality rods can cast further and catch better fish.

Fish appear in rivers, lakes, and oceans and have higher frequency at different times. There is an animation when a fish is on a tile, so the player can know where to cast their line.

There is a minigame event for catching fish where the player needs to line up a swaying bar within a box. Each time the player presses interact while to line is swaying gives a certain number of points based on how close it is to the center. If the number of points is greater than the number required to catch the fish, it is caught. If the number of points is less than required, there is a chance that the fish gets away. If the fish does not get away, the player has another chance to try to catch the fish. The points are cumulative. There is no time limit for each tug.

All Fish

A lower rarity means there is a lower chance of encountering this particular fish. Though there are many types of fish to catch, they are organized by group when put into the bag.

The temperature range is the water temperatures that the fish come out at. Water temperature is based on air temperature in the game. Something like for ever 1 degree of air temperature, add 0.75 degrees of water temperature. The closer to the middle of the range, the higher their chance of spawning is.

Mining
Mining is one of the three main tasks in (farmgame). To mine, the player must enter a dungeon and smash rocks with the hammer. The player can also dig the ground in dungeons with the shovel to reveal items. In dungeons, random battles happen where the player can gain money from winning battles.
^
Basics

Dungeons are randomly generated each time the player enters a new room. The top and bottom floors are not randomized. To mine, the player must smash rocks with a hammer. The player can also dig the ground to find items. To fight enemies, it is important to have equipment such as a sword and shield.

Since mining takes a lot of energy, it is important to have lots of restoration items. Luckily, ores can be exchanged for prizes outside of the dungeon and some of the prizes are restoration items. The main purpose of ores are to exchange for prizes. In mining worlds, the main source of income is battling, since ores cannot be easily sold.

Cooking

Cooking is another very important task in (farmgame). Not only is it a great way to increase the value of items, it is necessary to gain large quantities of healing items. Cooking takes place either in the farm of in a restaurant and requires ingredients, kitchenware, and time.

While cooking, there is a timer that counts up that has to do with how long it is taking to complete the current dish. Time passes at 1/6th of real time while cooking compared to the 1/60th normally. Once a dish has been completed for the first time, you can auto-cook which cooks one of what was made in the same time. This means that the player only has to manually cook dishes for the first time, though doing it multiple times to get faster times is a good idea. If cooking from a restaurant, all dishes are made manually.

^
Kitchen

The kitchen is a separate scene inside the farm and restaurant that allows the player to convert items into cooked dishes. To enter the kitchen, the player must interact with a door, which leads them there.

Cooking takes time, so some days may be taken up just by making large batches of dishes. Dishes generally restore a lot of energy and health and also tend to increase the value to higher that of the components combined.

Making a Dish

Items must be placed in the ingredients box before a recipe can be selected. Any forage or produce items can be placed in the ingredients box.

The player can read the recipe book to start making a dish. Once the player selects the dish, they can select "manual" or "auto". If the player selects "manual", they will create the dish through gameplay. If the player has already made this dish before, they can select "auto" to automatically create the dish in their fastest previous time. Selecting "auto" is the only way to create dishes in bulk. The player must have the necessary tools as well as items in the ingredients box to select a recipe.

Ingredients must be prepared during gameplay to qualify for a fastest time. Any leftover prepared ingredients are considered items and can be interacted with normally.

Restaurant

You can either own a restaurant or work part-time in one. Part-time work allows you to make money without providing your own ingredients. Each time the player works part time at a restaurant, he is given a recipe that is on the menu as well as his wage. As an owner, you make the menu as well as provide ingredients. In order to open your own restaurant, there must be one helper as the cook or one helper as the server. The player can do the other task, but obviously has to spend their day working at the restaurant. If there are two helper, one cook and one server, there must still be enough ingredients in the ingredients box to make dishes. The player needs to either drop off ingredients, or have a delivery helper do it.

There are two jobs in restaurants: Server, and Cook.

Server

As a server, your job is to make recommendations to customers, bring them food, and clean tables. As customers enter the restaurant, they will find a seat or wait in the waiting area if the restaurant is full. Customers will have different icons that appear above them to show their status. They can ask for a recommendation, order food, or want to pay. If they ask for a recommendation, you will see a customer profile that includes what types of foods they like, how hungry they are, and how much money they are willing to spend.

Cook

The cook's job is to cook food and place the food in the area where the server picks it up. When an order is placed, an icon appears with what is needed. Since the menu is known, the player can start preparing ingredients based on what could be ordered. What the player is paid is based on performance and wasting ingredients lowers pay.

Player Owned Shops

The player can own a few different types of shops. The amount of product that is sold is based on the popularity and population values of the town.

Food Kiosk

At the food kiosk, customers write their order at 8:00 and pick up their food at 16:00. The number of customers that can place an order is based on the satisfaction rating. Orders that get completed raise satisfaction, whereas orders that are missed lower it. People write their order down at 8:00 and pick it up at 16:00. When food is ready, it needs to be delivered back to the kiosk. Money is obtained by checking the cash box at the kiosk after 16:00.

The maximum number of customers can be limited as well. The shop can also be closed if the player doesn't plan on using it. It can be managed by a single delivery helper.

Farmer's Market Stand

You can own a shop in the farmer's market to sell produce. The farmer's market is only open on Saturdays from 10:00 - 14:00. Once your stand is set up with the items you want to sell showing, you're free to walk around and purchase things from other stands. If an item runs out, it may be replaced with something else. At 14:00, all stands close and you can collect the money that you have made from the cash box at your stand.

Fishing Derby

The fishing derby is a festival that is held where the player competes to get the most points. The derby starts at 6:00 and ends at 14:00. Once the derby is over, there is a cooking contest that has the fish that you caught as the main ingredient. At 14:00 the player is asked if the'd like to submit their catch. If the player doesn't want to submit their catch, they don't get teleported. If they visit the festival area before 19:00, they can still participate in the cooking contest.

The results for the derby is at 14:30 and the player is teleported to the festival area at 14:00 (Time skips half an hour) if they want to submit their catch. The player is asked if they'd like to participate in the cooking contest. If they say yes, they are given 4.5 hours to bring a fish-based dish. The player is teleported to the front of their house. The player must make their way back to town and enter the festival area before 19:00 with their dish. If the player does not have a fish-based dish to give, the festival will proceed as if the player did not want to bring a dish in the first place.

The goal of the fishing derby is to get the most points. Points are awarded based on the weight of fish, their rarity, and the number of fish caught. There are prizes awarded for first, second, and third. The player can fish anywhere. If the player participates in the cooking contest, they have a chance to win more prizes. All party members get healed to full health and energy after the cooking contest. Once the cooking contest is over, the player is teleported back to their house for 21:00. Options
Name Description
Auto Run Run without holding down the run button.
Music Enabled Toggle to have background music playing.
Music Volume The volume of the background music.
Sound Enabled Toggle to have sound effects play.
Sound Volume The volume of the sound effects.
Text Speed The speed that text cycles when talking to people and signs. Select from Slow, Normal, Fast, and Instant.
Brightness Adjust how bright the game is. Setting the value to lower covers the game with a black square with its opacity based on this number.
Auto Combine Automatically combine items of the same type when putting items in the bag. This averages the quality as if the player selected auto combine in the menu.
Menu Color Select the main color of menus.
Walk Through Walls Cheat Only available in testing. Allows the player to walk on any tile.

Helpers
^

NPC's
NPC's are the random characters that spawn throughout the game. They always have something to say and they sometimes ask questions that the player can answer. Their text can change based on certain variables.
NPC's are also in charge of the shops in town, as well as special shops in dungeons. They sometimes ask the player do bring them items in exchange for something. This is called a "request". This is the only way to get certain items and recipes.
